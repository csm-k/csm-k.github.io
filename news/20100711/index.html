 Prof. Kwang S. Kim selected as a winner of the "Top Scientist" (July 11, 2011)

Prof. Kwang Soo Kim (Chemistry) has been selected as a winner of the "Top Scientist" Award by the Ministry of Education, Science and Technology (MEST). The Ministry of Education, Science and Technology and the Korean Federation of Science and Technology Societies announced that three scientists, Prof. Kwang Soo Kim of POSTECH and two others from Seoul National University, would receive the "Top Scientist" Award. This prestigious award is given to scientists with remarkable achievements. It was established in 2003 to boost the sense of pride among scientists and to create an atmosphere where scientists can devote themselves to research. The winners will be awarded 300 million won as part of the award. MEST officials said "the winners for this year were selected for their world-class research achievements which contributed to the national development and the improvement of national welfare." Prof. Kim extended the realm of computational quantum chemistry to include nano, bio and information science by elucidating intermolecular interactions and self-assembly phenomena using quantum chemical methods. He also developed novel functional nanomaterials and devices, the most well-known of which is the world's first nanolens. He has been a pioneer in the field of molecular spintronics, with a number of outstanding scientific discoveries including the discovery of super optical phenomena exceeding the diffraction limit, the development of a method of large-scale synthesis of graphene monolayers based on self-assembly phenomena, and the discovery of the super magnetoresistance of graphene nanoribbons.
